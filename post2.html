The Rhythm of the Third Thadige

April 18, 2026

Of all the festivals throughout the year, Akshaya Tritiya and Gowri Habba are the ones that really stay with me. I never used to rank these festivals, but from a distance, they’re the traditions I find myself holding onto the most.

My mom has this way of celebrating every festival with so much care, following every tradition and ritual perfectly. But these two stand out to me because they feel so much like her. There’s a certain warmth she brings to them—the house filled with the smell of fresh jasmine and chandan, and the bright colors of the flowers and mangoes.

Especially with Akshaya Tritiya, everything feels so fresh. The way my mom celebrates this season is like a slow buildup. Right after Ugadi, she sets the Kalasa on the Thadige. It’s a dedicated process—she carefully changes the Kalasa every Tuesday and Friday, a rhythm I’ve always loved because it keeps that fresh, sacred energy alive in the house for weeks.

For those who didn't grow up with this, there’s a reason for the timing. The "Thadige" (the third day of the lunar month) after Ugadi is significant because it marks the real start of the summer transition. By the time the third Thadige—Akshaya Tritiya—arrives, the "Akshaya" part kicks in, which literally means "never diminishing" or "eternal."

The ritual of the Kalasa represents the goddess Gowri staying in the house, and refreshing it is a way of keeping that hospitality alive until the final celebration. By the time it is time to move the Kalasa, the air is thick with the scent of jasmine.

In the peak of summer, her mango rice, payasa, kosambri, and panaka are the perfect combination, especially with the house smelling of fresh jasmine.

Mom’s "No-Secret" Mango Rice

Now, don’t expect a "secret ingredient" or a list of gourmet tips here. My mom isn’t really the type to have kitchen hacks or hidden spices—she saves her tips and tricks for everything else in life!

Her mango rice is just... mango rice. It’s the classic version everyone knows. Here is exactly how she makes it:

Mango Rice Ingredients

  • • 2 cups cooked rice (grains separate)
  • • 1 large raw mango, grated
  • • The Tempering: Oil, mustard seeds, chana dal, urad dal
  • • A handful of peanuts
  • • Green chilies & curry leaves
  • • A pinch of turmeric and salt

Method

1. Heat the oil and add the mustard seeds. Add the chana dal, urad dal, and peanuts. Fry them until they are perfectly crunchy and golden.

2. Throw in the green chilies and curry leaves.

3. Add the grated mango and turmeric, sautéing just enough to take the raw edge off.

4. Fold in the rice and salt until everything is a bright, beautiful yellow.

It’s simple, tangy, and tastes exactly like home.
